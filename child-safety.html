Child Safety Standards

Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

Realr is committed to the safety and protection of children. We have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or any form of child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) on our platform. This document outlines our standards, policies, and procedures for preventing, detecting, and responding to CSAE.

1. Age Requirement

Realr requires all users to be at least 13 years of age. Users must confirm their age during account registration. Accounts suspected of belonging to individuals under 13 will be immediately suspended and removed.

2. Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on Realr:

  • Any sexual content involving minors, including images, text, illustrations, or any other media
  • Sharing, distributing, or soliciting child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  • Grooming behavior — building trust with a minor for the purpose of sexual abuse or exploitation
  • Soliciting sexual acts or images from minors
  • Sextortion or coercion of minors
  • Trafficking or promoting the trafficking of minors
  • Any communication that sexualizes minors
  • Arranging real-world meetings with minors for the purpose of sexual exploitation

3. Detection and Reporting

Realr employs the following measures to detect and respond to CSAE content:

  • Community flagging: All users can flag posts and report inappropriate content. Posts that receive multiple flags are automatically hidden from view pending review.
  • Content review: Flagged content is reviewed and any content that violates these standards is immediately removed.
  • Account termination: Users found to be engaging in CSAE-related activity will have their accounts permanently terminated without warning.
  • Law enforcement reporting: Any identified CSAM or CSAE activity will be reported to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) via the CyberTipline, and to relevant law enforcement authorities, in compliance with applicable laws.

4. User Reporting

Users who encounter any content or behavior that may constitute CSAE should:

  • Use the in-app flag/report feature to immediately report the content
  • Contact us directly at contact@realrapp.com with details of the incident
  • Contact law enforcement if they believe a child is in immediate danger

All reports of CSAE are treated with the highest priority and urgency.

5. Data Retention for Safety

While Realr's posts are ephemeral by design, we reserve the right to preserve and retain any content, account data, and associated metadata when required for the investigation of CSAE incidents, compliance with legal obligations, or cooperation with law enforcement.

6. Cooperation with Authorities

Realr will fully cooperate with law enforcement agencies investigating CSAE. This includes providing account information, content data, IP addresses, device identifiers, and any other relevant information when legally required or when we believe in good faith that a child's safety is at risk.

7. Employee and Developer Standards

All individuals involved in the development and operation of Realr are expected to uphold these child safety standards. Any failure to adhere to these standards will result in immediate removal from the project.

8. Ongoing Commitment

We are committed to continuously improving our child safety practices. This includes staying informed about evolving threats, updating our detection methods, and working with industry partners and organizations dedicated to child safety. We regularly review and update these standards to ensure they reflect current best practices.

9. Contact

For questions about these standards or to report CSAE concerns, please contact us at contact@realrapp.com.
